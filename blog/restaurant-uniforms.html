Custom Restaurant Uniforms

The complete guide to dressing your team

Your restaurant's appearance speaks volumes about your brand. Custom embroidered uniforms create a professional, cohesive look that impresses customers and builds brand recognition. Here's everything you need to know about outfitting your restaurant team.

Why Custom Restaurant Uniforms Matter

✅ Benefits of Custom Uniforms:
  • Professional appearance that builds customer trust
  • Brand visibility with every interaction
  • Team unity and pride in appearance
  • Easy identification for customers
  • Differentiation from competitors

Uniform Options by Role

Executive Chef / Head Chef

Traditional double-breasted chef coat in white or black. Embroidered with your restaurant logo on the left chest. Consider adding the chef's name for a personal touch.

Line Cooks / Kitchen Staff

More practical options like chef jackets that can take a beating. Durable fabrics that withstand frequent washing. Logo embroidery on the back is popular for kitchen visibility.

Servers

Button-up shirts, polo shirts, or vests depending on your restaurant's style. Embroidery on the left chest is standard and professional.

Hosts / Hostesses

Often match or complement the server attire. Polos or button-ups work well. First impressions matter at the door!

Bar Staff

Black shirts are popular for bars. Consider embroidered vests for a sharp look. Aprons with logo for back bar staff.

Choosing Fabrics

  • Cotton blends: Breathable, comfortable, easy to care for
  • Polyester: Wrinkle-resistant, durable, quick-drying
  • Mix fabrics: Best of both worlds — comfort and durability
💡 Tip: Kitchen staff should look for fabrics that can withstand high temperatures and frequent washing.

Branding Options

  • Left chest logo: Standard, professional (3"-4")
  • Full back design: Great for kitchen staff visibility
  • Name + title: Personal touch for front of house
  • Apron branding: Subtle but effective

How to Order

  1. Determine needs: Count how many of each role you need to outfit
  2. Choose styles: Select uniform styles that match your brand
  3. Provide logo: Send us your logo in a vector format
  4. Get a quote: We'll provide pricing with volume discounts
  5. Production: Typically 2-3 weeks for custom orders
